The Cipher Gate
Welcome to the Entropy Unit recruitment challenge. Your first test is a basic cryptography puzzle. Below you'll find an encoded message using a simple substitution cipher. Your task is to decode it.

The cipher used is a Caesar cipher with a shift of 3 positions forward in the alphabet (A→D, B→E, etc.). Numbers and special characters remain unchanged. Only letters are shifted.
Encoded Message:
Wkhuh'v d vhfuhw phvvdjh klgghq lq wklv whaw. Brx qhhg wr ghfrgh lw xvlqj wkh vkliw fbskhuv. Wkh dqvzhu lv wkh zrug wkdw xqorfnv wkh qhaw ohyho.
Need a hint? (Click to reveal)
The Caesar cipher shifts each letter by a fixed number of positions. For a shift of 3, A becomes D, B becomes E, C becomes F, and so on. At the end of the alphabet, it wraps around: X becomes A, Y becomes B, Z becomes C.

Try decoding the first word "Wkhuh'v" with a shift of 3. What does it become?
